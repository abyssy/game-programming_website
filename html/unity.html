Birth


In 2004, Unity was born in Amsterdam, Denmark.

In 2005, it was headquartered in the United States, San Francisco, and released the Unity 1.0 version.

At first it can only be applied to the MAC platform, mainly for WEB projects and VR development.

At this time it is inconspicuous, until the launch of Windows version in 2008, and the beginning to support iOS and Wii.

It stood out and followed the trend of mobile games and became hot. In 2009, Unity registered the number of people has reached 35,000, topped the 2009 game engine of the top five.

In 2010, Unity began to support android, continue to spread the influence.

Its support in 2011, PS3 and XBOX360, you can see the completion of the construction of the whole platform.




Feature


Unity has an emphasis on portability.

Unity allows specification of texture compression and resolution settings for each platform that the game engine supports, and provides support for bump mapping, reflection mapping, parallax mapping, screen space ambient occlusion (SSAO), dynamic shadows using shadow maps, render-to-texture and full-screen post-processing effects.

Unity's graphics engine's platform diversity can provide a shader with multiple variants and a declarative fallback specification, allowing Unity to detect the best variant for the current video hardware and, if none are compatible, to fall back to an alternative shader that may sacrifice features for performance.

Unity is notable for its ability to target games to multiple platforms.

Within a project, developers have control over delivery to mobile devices, web browsers, desktops, and consoles.
